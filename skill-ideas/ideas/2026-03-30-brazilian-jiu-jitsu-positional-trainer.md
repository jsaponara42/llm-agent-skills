# Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu Positional Trainer and Game

## Problem
Most BJJ drilling tools are static, text-heavy, or too slow to feel like live positional sparring where reactions must happen quickly.

## Pain Point
Practitioners struggle to mentally rehearse realistic positional sequences because they do not get fast, move-by-move prompts that stay logically consistent with the current position.

## Description
A live voice skill that starts from a declared BJJ position, announces where the user is, then makes a realistic move from that position. The user responds with their reaction, and the skill continues the sequence as a positional game. It should prioritize a smaller, faster model for natural conversational speed. The skill should support uploaded flow charts for specific positions so transitions and move options remain realistic and constrained by the provided positional logic.

## Value Driver
This creates a fast, practical way to train decision-making and positional awareness between classes, helping users think through reactions in realistic sequences without needing a partner on the mat.

## Optional Notes
- Users: BJJ practitioners who want quick mental reps and positional reaction training.
- Current workaround: Solo visualization, static instructionals, or slow chat interactions that break realism.
- Success signal: Users can complete rapid voice rounds where each move sequence remains positionally valid and feels like real rolling.
- Scope boundaries: Focus on positional flow and realistic transitions; not full biomechanics coaching or medical/safety advice.
