### Alex Hormozi - $100M Money Models - Attraction Offers

# Overview

Attraction Offers get customers by offering something free or at a discount. Often, they also make money by offering a better deal at a higher price.

### Each attraction offer, and offer specifics:

###### 1. Win your money back: You set a goal for the customer and tell them how to reach it. If they reach it then they qualify to get their money back or get it back as store credit.

- 3 options: Get results, take actions, or both.

- To make this work you have to make the results and actions simple to track

- Results: Here, no matter what they do, if the customer gets the result, they win their money back. For example: Making $X/month, Getting Y customers, Losing Z pounds etc. Basically they bet on their own ability to reach the goal.

- Actions: Hold them accountable for doing actions instead of getting results. For example - attend all sessions, calls, meetings, log progress, take pictures, do assigned homework, etc. Here they bet on their ability to follow directions.

- Both: By setting a good goal and showing them how to reach it, they have a fighting chance of achieving the goal. Here they bet on their ability to follow direction and that your directions will get them the result.

### Examples:
- Business to consumer offers: Free 28 day blueprint
Deposit X dollars and get it all back if you... Attend all consulting calls, post your progress in the group once per week, journal daily in our app, attend yuor feedback session and your transformation session, Attend your feedback session and your transformation session. (Calls and meetings become opportunities to make more offers)

- B2B offer: 5 customers in 5 days free challenge
Deposit X dollars and get it all back if you... Send 100 messages per day, report stats on messages sent, attend daily training, post finished homework in the group attend the 5 day consulting call (where you'll offer more, better, or new products and services)

- Physical product offer: Put 1,000,000 miles on your car and get a free car
Get a free car if you... Buy a car from us, drive the car 1,000,000 miles, turn it in, take pictures and be in a press release, we'll credit your original purchase price towards your next car.

### Important notes:
- This works with new, current, and previous customers
- It works well with stuff people regularly start and quit. It keeps motivation during early pains of learning
- This offer makes money. Most wont qualify even under realistic conditions. Those who do qualify often stay customers. They can only stay as customers if there's something else to buy - so have an upsell ready to apply to their winnings.
- Around 10% of customers ask for their money back. If you can't handle that, don't do this.
- You can offer store credit instead of cash.
- Don't take blood money - if a customer asks for their money back even if they lost, just give it to them.


### How to create criteria:
1. Easy to track - train them on exactly what they need to do. Bonus points if they already do it (for example, phones already track steps. Word processors already track word count. cameras automatically date photos)

2. Get customers results - Make criteria likely to get them desired resutls. Be REALISTIC - if you think the criteria look TOO EASY, you're probably CLOSE to realistic and you have to get easier. (attend meetings, workouts, watch videos, etc.) Whatever stuff the best customers do to get the best results. Make everyone do it and they'll get great results too.

3. Advertises the Business - make advertising the business part of your criteria. post about participation. tagging in social media, referring or leaving reviews and testimonials.


### How to apply store credit - When customers win - offer to apply it to a longer period of time or to a bulk package.

### All meetings and calls provide opportunities to make more offers. Make check in meetings part of your money-back criteria whenever possible. Make all meetings required. They are the best opportunities for upsells.

### Make everyone a winner - Promote as if they will only get back if they meet criteria. But about halfway through, make the next offer AS IF THEY ALREADY WON. " I know you're trying to hit this short term goal, but what is your long term goal?...Great to hear...YOu get its about long term results...Tell you what, to show you how much I want you to hit that long term goal, I'll credit this program toward the next one whether you hit the short term or not. How does that sound?"

### At the end, let the "losers" win - If someone refuses the first upsell and fails,  still upsell them again.  "Don't worry about it. You started. That's the biggest win. Even though yuo didn't hit your goal, you hit OURS - which was finishing what you started. We're in this for the long haul, so we're going to credit your entire deposit towards staying long term. You get your money back, and we can still hit your goal. How does that sound?" You'll turn the frown upside down and they'll love you for it.

### Summary and tips:
- The real money comes from the people who succeed with it and you have something else to offer them.
- Win your money back is great for businesses that require customers to put in continuous effort to get their ideal outcome.
- Good because you get: loads of cash up front, more customers to say yes because lower risk, massive results for customers, more long-term customers, they advertise your offer so you get more customers.
- Make refund criteria easy to track, aligned with customer goals, and helpful for the business.
- Put store credit toward another, preferably more expensive offer.
- Make everyone a winner in private so they're surprised and grateful when you make an upsell offer.



#####2. Giveaways: You advertise a chance to win a big prize in exchange for contact information and anything else you want. After picking a winner you offer everyone else the big prize at a discounted price.

- To run a giveaway offer you: Pick a grand prize, pick your promotional offer, ask for contact info and other eligibility criteria, pick what actions you want entrants to take to qualify for the big prize, but the giveaway on a deadline to add urgency, announce the grand prize winner and contact everyone else

- Pick a grand prize: Make your grand prize the thing you want everyone to buy. Assign a monetary value and use it as your price anchor. If you sell $5000 of value for $2000 - advertise the $5000 value

- Pick your promo offer: Enhance your core offer with a discount, a bonus, or minor change from grand prize to ethically justify a price reduction. Bigger discount makes offer more attractive using grand prize as anchor. It gets you qualified leads because you offer what they already showed interest in at a discount. Call your promo offer  - the thing you sell to everyone else - whatever you want (scholarship, gift card, credit, vouchers, etc.)

- Ask for contact info: Ask for permission to contact them any way you please for follow up promotions. Survey for prize eligibilityand ask them to take qualifying actions.

- Eligibility: Ask if they're a fit for the products, or character/need based questions.

- Qualifying actions: Get them to promote giveaway more, attend call, make a post, enter group, etc.

- Put the giveaway on a deadline to add urgency: 3 to 7 days from start of promotion. Update entrants daily. Once a day is fine. Provide value along with the countdown. Show everyone benefits of the grand prize an dhow excited they should be . Refer everyone to social proof. Keep the hype alive!

- Announce grand prize winner and start contacting everyone else: Announce grand prize publicly, and message everyone else who qualifies for your core offer privately. EVERYONE ELSE QUALIFIES FOR PROMO OFFER. Ask to schedule a call withthem because they qualified for something else. Make the promo expire in 7 days. Create a second countdown - show benefits, more social proof, more valuable stuff. Give them a way to book a call to claim the promo offer.

- Explain the cost-to-value using their discount: Make core offer discount equal to 10%-30% of gross margins. If we advertise $5k grand prize value with $2k pricetag, everyone else gets it for $1,800. By comparing the value of the thing to what they pay, a 10% discount becomes a 64% difference in cost to value.

## EXAMPLES:
- Dentist: Free perfect smile giveaway. Grand prize: free set of invisible braces - $6000 retail price. Promo offer: $2000 gift card for braces.

- Physical products: Dog food example. Grand prize: Free year of organic dog food - $1000 retail price. Promo offer: $300 gift card for dog food only usable with 1 year subscription.

- Services offer: Free ultimate giveaway. Grand prize: Free 1 year package - $5000 retail. Promo: $2000 voucher redeemable toward 1 year service agreement.

- Consulting offer: Free 16 week turnaround giveaway. Grand prize: 16 week turnaround - $12,000 retail price, Promo offer: $6000 partial scholarship.

## IMPORTANT NOTES:

- Consult legal about how to structure. Make the grand prize, qualifications to win clear in the rules. Make clear that more than one person can win. Ask legal about the rest.

- Eligibility criteria to get more customers to buy: Why should we pick you? Why this program? Why now? Why does this matter to you? Why is this important to you? What is your goal? That being said, more criteria = fewer entrants, but more qualified. Find the sweet spot.

- If your gievaway doesn't work, it means your grand prize wasn't enough. If nobody bites, give away something better. Or at least better for the audience.

- Give away two prizes for twice the leads: Tell everyone that if someone they refer wins the grand prize, they win one too.

- Scarcity, scarcity, scarcity: Limit giveaway by time, number of entries, or both. Match how many people you let enter with the number of people you have the time and resources to connect with inside 7 days. Any more is a waste.

- Urgency, urgency, urgency: ALWAYS HAVE DEADLINES. Deadlines for entering clear in ads. Deadline for claim clear. When they do claim, schedule call same day or next day. Once they win, tell people how long they have to use it - hours, but up to 5 days.

- Have donwnsells available: Some wont or cant buy promo offer even with discount. At the start of the call let them know they qualified for 2 prizes. And that you'll help them find which way makes most sense for them. Present promo first. If they don't take it, offer same discount by percentage on any other product you have that makes sense for them.

- If you have a recurring revenue business: APPLY THE DISCOUNT OVER THE LONGEST PERIOD OF TIME THEY'LL AGREE TO. Then set up billing to bill normal rates after discounted period ends.

### Summary and tips:
- Pick a grand prize people want
- Use the info from everyone who enters to improve the value or pain point your solving
- Book appointments with everyone else to claim your promo offer. Use whatever "reason why" feels good to you.
- Expiration dates make people more likely to claim.



##### 3. Decoy offers: you advertize a free or discounted offer. When the lead asks to learn more, you also present a more valuable premium offer. The premium offer includes more features, benefits, bonuses, guarantees, and so on.

- Give customers what they want now so you can give them what they need later.

- You have to know what gets results for customers better than they do. This makes the premium offer the clear solution. Making the premium offer the clear solution is what decoy is all about.

- By putting your decoy offers and premium offers side by side, leads can see how much more valuable your premium offer is.

- If they take the decoy it is also good. It gives you time to upgrade them rather than losing them.

- Steps for a decoy offer:
1. Advertise a lesser, smaller, or simpler version of your premium offer as a decoy
2. When leads engage, offer both options, but emphasize the premium one.


### EXAMPLES:

Lemonade stand (physical products):
- Attraction offer: "free week of lemonade" or "$1 week of lemonade"
- Decoy option: "you can have this water, powdered lemon, corn syrup, or...."
- Premium option: "Organic, all natural, vegan, etc. etc. Shipped straight to your door. Never waste time coming to store again. Feeling amazing. Other flavors."

Float tank (service):
- Attraction offer: "Free 6 week stress release" or "$6 6 week stress release"
- Decoy option: One float per month with at home do it yourself stress relief exercises
- Premium opiton: Two times per week floats for 6 weeks, 1:1 consulting, journal, sleep routine. Satisfaction guaranteed.

Gym offer (Local business):
- Attraction offer: "Free 21-day transformation" or "$21 21 day transformation"
- Decoy option: Workouts done in a [skool.com](http://skool.com/) group once a day. General nutrition plan. Can watch recordings. No support. No guarantees.
- Premium option: Unlimited workouts. Personalized nutrition plan. 1:1 accountability. Results guaranteed or you get another 21 days free


### IMPORTANT NOTES:

- How to make your decoy offer: offer fewer components, older models, or fewer personalized versions of your premium offer. REmove any guarantees. This only needs to get leads to engage.

- Advertise benefits, not features: We want to sell them on the DREAM OUTCOME. Give specific product details in the presentation, not the advertising.

- You can advertise discounts in 4 ways: Percentage off, absolute amount off, free portions (3 months free), total package (one year for $900 - used to be $1200)

- Make the contrast HUGE: Make the decoy option as basic as is reasonable. Make premium option as awesome as possible. Bigger the contrast, the better the deal and the more customers will take it.

- Discount offers have higher show-up rates than free offers: If you have low show up rates for appointments - try a discount offer instead - especially for important businesses where you have a high cost of someone not showing up (doctors, lawyers, dentists, etc.)

- If possible, present premium offer first: Present premium first, decoy offer in back pocket.. if they come asking specifically for decoy....

- Get them to give you permission to sell them: You are legally required to present the decoy if they ask about it. Ask "are you here for the free stuff or the lasting results?". If they say "results" you can present premium offer. If they say "free" present decoy, immediately contrasted by premium. After presenting both, ask "which do you think will get you to your goal faster?" or "which would you prefer: XXX less valuable benefit, or YYY more valuable benefit 1,2,3..?" At this point they'll have to say premium, and you can move forward in the sale mutually agreeing it's the best thing for them.

- When making your premium offer, get excited about it: Your excitement motivates people to take the options that will give them the most value.

- Use the assumed close. Operate from a position of "this is what everyone does. This is just a formality. Let me get your ID and credit card so you can get the value."

- Surprise benefit (optional): If someone takes the decoy you can throw something in from premium to surprise them for free. "Hey I'm gonna throw this in, even though its part of our premium offer just because I want you to get great results." This builds goodwill, overdelivers, and increases the chance they take upsells later. Remember - they're still leads!

## Summary and tips:

- You can still make money from leads who pick the decoy option. you'll learn the best way to deliver your decoy product and maximize upsells from it.

- Expect to make money fast. If you're not, then make the contrast between offers larger.


##### 4. Buy X get Y free: You offer customers free stuff in exchange for buying other stuff for money. The more free stuff and the higher it's value, the more people buy.

- When you have more than one item, you can make the discount value alrge enough that it covers the price of more stuff. For example sell one t-shirt for $30 and give two away free instead of 3 t-shirts for $10 each.

### EXAMPLES:
- Buy 1 Get 2 free (physical products): One pair of boots $200, Buy X get Y free offer: buy one $600 pair, get two pairs free. End result: they still buy 3 pairs of $200 boots for a total of $600

- 3 versions. 18 months of services: Good: "Buy 12 months get 6 free ($1800)", Better: "Buy 9 months get 9 free ($1800)", Best: "Buy 6 months, get 12 free ($1800)". Everyone pays the same price for the same service, but more free stuff is most compelling.

### IMPORTANT NOTES:

- Buy X get Y free gets people to buy more AND provides more value

- Raise prices before giving stuff away to preserve profit: Permanently raise prices to accommodate the discount. Plenty of people may still take your raised prices a la carte and break your limiting beliefs around pricing.

- Buy X get Y free works better if you have more free stuff than paid stuff: Buy ten get two free is not as strong as buy 2 get 10 free. It seems obvious but people don't do it. More free than you ask people to buy.

- More free cheaper things can work better than fewer free expensive things: For example, buy 1 shirt get 3 socks free, better than buy 1 shirt get 1 shirt free. Sometimes more cheaper things works better than fewer expensive things.

- Rather than offer a 33% discount, try buy one get two free

- Do not make offers like this if you can't manage money: while you may get a whole years worth of payments in a month, make sure you can deliver for the whole year.

- Make this offer to existing customers for fast cash: Limit the offer to 10% of your current customers. Good cash pop and keeps recurring cashflow healthly.

- Don't worry. Prepaid customers keep buying stuff. So keep selling it to them!: Speaking from experience, these are the people who spend the most money. Give the other offers to buy and they will. They prepaid months ago. Their wallets have been "refreshed" with new money that is dying to make it into your pocket. Don't get in the way!

- If customers only buy once, make them buy BIG. Make the one purchase as big as possible.

### Summary and tips:

- Buy X get Y free works for stuff that makes sense to buy more of or get longer access to.

- Buy X Get Y free can lengthen the amount of time customers stay. If normal customrs stay for 3 months, buy 2 get 2 free will keep them for 4 months, or whatever you set it at. This give you more opportunities to make more offers and provide more value.

- Keep selling customers who prepay long durations! They are the most likely customers to buy again!


##### 5. Pay less now or pay more later: You give people a choice to pay full price later or pay a discounted price now and get additional bonuses.

- This works well because we remove all risks from the customer. They pay later AND only if they like it. So it combines benefits of delayed payment and satisfaction guarantee. Almost anyone will agree to pay later if they are satisfied. But onec they agree to pay later, you can get them to pay now with hefty discounts and valuable bonuses.

- Pay later option allows you to advertise FREE since they can choose to pay or not. Free offer gets their card on file. If they pick this option and hate the product they can cancel any time before the charge goes through.

- If they accept pay later, we make a follow up offer to pay now - pay now provides a 20-50% discount and greater bonuses. Easy to pay since their card is on file.

- Be prepared to upsell to these customers later


### EXAMPLES:

Find your first real estate deal - free 3 day workshop
- Pay later: $0 for 3 day workshop, they get billed $500 unless they cancel
- Pay now: $299 for 3 day workshop plus recordings. 1:1 call with certified distressed property expert. Plus printed materials to use (delivered at the workshop)
- Upsell: $30,000 to take you through every other step of closing your first deal within 6 months. Plus legal templates, advisor to vet the investment. Inspection checklist, etc.

Local business service - trim your hedges for free
- Pay later: $0 lawn cut + hedges then $599 after
- Pay now: $369 lawn cut + hedges + lawn treatment
- Upsell: $199/month lawncare services
The rep comes to the house, makes the estimat, and offers both options, then upsells after the work is done

Physical products: 14-day clothing trial
- Pay later: $0 now. Get it. Then billed $149 in 14 days
- Pay now: $97 for clothing + accessory that goes with it
- Upsell: The dress coes with an offer for a monthly subscription to more clothes like this.

### IMPORTANT NOTES:

- Promise a clear yes/no result: and make sure you can deliver within your time frame. Otherwise they will ask not to be billed. If you promise to decrease someones shoulder pain have them rate their pain 1-10 before your service, then ask them later to rate it. Keep the promise simple, clear, and measurable to avoid unnecessary cancellations.

- Make a conditional satisfaction guarantee: People can only cancel if they qualify. Show up to training etc. Can't say they we aren't good if they never tried it. Track conditions necessary to qualify (attendance, showing up to appointment, turning in data etc.) Make the criteria what people do to get the most value out of the product. Win-win

- Bonuses for pay now option

- Optimizing pay now and pay later offer: if too many people take pay later, discount pay now more, add better bonuses, or both. If too many people take pay now, do the opposite.

- If more than 10% of pay later cancel their payment: You promised too much, the guarantee conditions are too low, or the price is too high. Some people will cancel, that's okay.

- This works for recurring businesses too: You give them the option to pay a higher ongoing rate 30 days later or they pay less today and keep the lower rate for good, plus some bonuses.

- If you run events/workshops/presentations - hint at your next offer early: Imply that everyone buys the next thing/upsell casually. For example in a nutrition consultation "everyone wants to know what supplements to buy. We'll get there - I promise. please pay attention to the nutrition section it matters more."

### Summary and tips:

- Offer customers pay now AFTER they accept pay later option.

- If they choose pay now they get the discount and bonuses INSTEAD of the guarantee

- Give extra attention to those who claim they haven't recieved what was promised before the cancellation deadline.
